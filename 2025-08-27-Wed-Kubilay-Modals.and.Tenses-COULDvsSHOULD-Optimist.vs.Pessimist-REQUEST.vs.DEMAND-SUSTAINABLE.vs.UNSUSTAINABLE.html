Conversational Fillers & Expressions

  • "If not now, then when?" - A common saying used when you feel a decision needs to be made now, rather than later.
  • "We've grown bored" - An expression that shows you have been bored over a period of time.
  • "He's a yes man" - An expression for a person who always agrees to what others ask, even if it's impossible.

New Vocabulary

  • Coincidence: A situation in which two or more things happen at the same time or in the same place, but with no plan or connection between them.
  • Serendipity: A lucky coincidence or a fortunate accident. It's when you find something good or useful by chance. The adjective form is serendipitous.
  • Optimist: A person who sees the good in things.
  • Pessimist: A person who sees the worst in things.
  • Demanding: Asking for a lot of work or effort.
  • Projecting: Transferring your feelings onto others.
  • Sustainable: Something you can continue doing for a long time without problems.
  • Unsustainable: Something that cannot be continued for a long time.
  • Increasing: Becoming greater in size, amount, or degree.
  • Eloquent: Speaking or writing in a clear and effective way.
  • Fluctuate: To change or vary often in an irregular way.

Present Perfect Continuous Errors

What was said: "I have already learning."

Correction: "I have already been learning."

(Remember the full structure for Present Perfect Continuous: have/has + been + verb-ing.)

Teacher's Note: The main challenge with PPC is consistently remembering the 'been + -ing' structure and using correct prepositions. Keep practicing!

Verb Agreements (Auxiliary Verb)

Remember to use the correct auxiliary verb ('have' or 'has') with each subject:

  • I have (I've)
  • You have (You've)
  • We have (We've)
  • They have (They've)
  • He has (He's)
  • She has (She's)
  • It has (It's)
