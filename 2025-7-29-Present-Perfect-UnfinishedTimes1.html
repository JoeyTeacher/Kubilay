"Let's catch up"

To meet someone you haven't seen in a while and find out what they have been doing.

  • "Let's get a drink sometime and catch up."

"To bump into someone"

To see someone you know unexpectedly. (e.g. at the shops, in the park, etc.)

"That must..."

To make an assumption or guess about someone's feelings.

  • "That must make her very happy."

This sound is very important for saying contractions correctly.

Practice saying it softly, like the sound in **V**ery, but at the end of the word.

Introducing the IPA Chart

The IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) chart shows all the sounds in English. We will practice this more in the future. It's a great tool for understanding and correctly producing sounds.

What is Voiced vs. Unvoiced?

  • Voiced sounds use the vocal cords. Put your hand on your throat when you say a voiced sound like /v/. You should feel a strong vibration.
  • Unvoiced sounds do not use the vocal cords. When you say an unvoiced sound like /f/, you will only feel air moving from your mouth, with no vibration in your throat.

Tip: The /v/ sound is voiced!

The /v/ sound, as in I've and have, is a voiced sound. If you make it unvoiced, it becomes the /f/ sound, as in favor or find. This is a common mistake that is important to practice.
