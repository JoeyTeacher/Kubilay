Making Guesses & Expressing Certainty

Here's a quick look at how to express different levels of certainty:

  • I'm sure / It is: Use these for 100% certainty. For example, "I'm sure it's over 1,500 kilometers."
  • It might be: Use this for possibility (around 30-60% certainty). For example, "It might be as much as 1,700 kilometers."
  • I will: Use this for future actions you are 100% certain about. For example, "I will see Akin tomorrow."
  • I might: Use this for future actions that are possible but not certain. For example, "I might see Akin tomorrow."

New Vocabulary

  • demanding: a task or a person that requires a lot of effort or attention.
  • work-life balance: the amount of time you spend working versus the amount of time you spend relaxing and socializing.
  • opposite schedules: when two people work at different times and rarely have time off together.
  • multitasking: being able to do many things at the same time.
  • all at once: a synonym for multitasking, meaning to do everything at the same time.
  • assistance: help or support.

Correction: I've gone on a lot of holidays.

(To talk about going on a holiday, we use the verb 'go'.)

Review: The difference between "been" and "gone"

We use "been" when a person has gone to a place and returned, but we use "gone" when they have gone to a place and are still there.

Teacher's Note: Pay attention to the subtle differences between PPS and PPC, especially when describing actions that have a duration or a clear result.

A Quick Clarification: Using "Too"

We had a good discussion about the difference between "many" and "too many" today. Remember that using "too" with an adjective or adverb often means it's a negative or excessive amount.

  • "This coffee is hot." (Neutral statement)
  • "This coffee is too hot." (Negative, it's hotter than you want it to be. You can't drink it.)
  • "I've eaten a lot of chocolate." (Neutral statement)
  • "I've been eating too much chocolate." (Negative, it's more than you should eat. You might have a stomach ache!)
