Present Perfect Simple (PPS)

You also used PPS for completed actions or states up to now:

  • Your hair hasn't been cut very well. (Describes a completed action with a result in the present.)

  • She's just made a cake. (Emphasizes a recently completed action.)
  • I haven't eaten anything yet since I woke up. (Connects a past action to the present result.)

PPS is crucial for talking about experiences, completed actions relevant to the present, and introducing news.

Approximators

You learned and practiced how to use these words to talk about approximate times or quantities:

  • I've been going there for almost 4 years. (Shows a duration that is close to a certain number.)
  • I woke up at around 9 AM. (Gives an approximate time.)

Using approximators makes your speech sound more natural and precise when exact figures aren't necessary.

Verb Agreements (Auxiliary Verb)

Remember to use the correct auxiliary verb ('have' or 'has') with each subject:

  • I have (I've)
  • You have (You've)
  • We have (We've)
  • They have (They've)
  • He has (He's)
  • She has (She's)
  • It has (It's)
