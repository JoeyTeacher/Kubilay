Grammar Review: Present Perfect Continuous for Recent Results üìù

Here's a quick review of how to use the Present Perfect Continuous to talk about actions that have recently finished, and there's clear evidence or a result now. This is a very useful way to describe how past actions affect the present!

Present Perfect Continuous for Recent Results

We use the Present Perfect Continuous (have/has been + Verb-ing) to explain the *cause* of a present situation. The action has just stopped, but its effects are visible now.

  • Example: "You look tired. Have you been sleeping well?" (The tiredness is the evidence of not sleeping well.)
  • Example: "The ground is wet. It has been raining." (The wet ground is the evidence of recent rain.)
  • Example: "My eyes are red because I've been watching too much TV." (Red eyes are the evidence of watching too much TV.)
