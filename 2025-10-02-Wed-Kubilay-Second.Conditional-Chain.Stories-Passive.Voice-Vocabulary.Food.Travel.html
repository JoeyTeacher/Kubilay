Second Conditional (Imaginary) Rule

For unlikely or imagined situations, you must use were for the verb 'to be' in the 'if' clause, even with 'I' or 'he' (the subjunctive mood). This is formal and required for B1 exams!

  • Correct: "If I were a genius, I would be a scientist."
  • Correct: "If he were angry with me, I would probably leave."

Key Conditional Phrasing

Grammar Review: Conditional Tenses üìù

This is the critical difference in structure. You must match the verb tense in the result clause to the tense in the 'if' clause.

First Conditional (Possible Future)

Condition (If Clause) Result (Action Clause)
If + Simple Present will / won't + Base Verb
Example: If I win money, Result: I will buy an island.

Second Conditional (Imaginary/Unlikely)

Condition (If Clause) Result (Action Clause)
If + Simple Past would / wouldn't + Base Verb
Example: If I bought an island, Result: I would call my friends.

The "Muscle Man" Chain (Second Conditional)
