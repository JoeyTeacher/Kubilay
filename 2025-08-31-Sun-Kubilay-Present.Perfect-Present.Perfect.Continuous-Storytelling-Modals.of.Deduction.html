Present Perfect Continuous (PPC)

We use PPC to talk about actions that started in the past and are still happening, or have recently stopped but have a visible result now. This helps you discuss ongoing experiences and activities.

  • "I've been studying English since the morning."
  • "My rent prices have been increasing."
  • "My focus has been decreasing."

Present Perfect Simple (PPS)

We use PPS for completed actions that have an effect on the present, or for experiences that happened at an unspecified time in the past. This is great for talking about life experiences.

  • "We've already bought our flight tickets."
  • "I've never climbed a mountain."
  • "I've always wanted to speak English fluently."

Modals of Deduction

We use modal verbs to make guesses about situations. Using them correctly shows you can express a degree of certainty about what you think is true.

  • "He must be hungry." (A strong guess based on evidence.)
  • "She might have been making a cake." (A weaker guess, a possibility.)
  • "He could be a paramedic." (A general possibility or option.)

New Vocabulary

  • Serendipity: Finding something good or pleasant by chance. You used this perfectly to describe the coincidence with your friend and his friend.
  • Fluctuates: To change or vary, especially continuously and unpredictably. You used this to talk about your work schedule.
  • Puddle: A small pool of liquid, especially rain, on the ground. We used this when describing the picture of the naughty child.
  • Cuisine: A style or method of cooking, especially as characteristic of a particular country or region. You used this when describing different types of food.

Verb Agreements (Auxiliary Verb)

Remember to use the correct auxiliary verb ('have' or 'has') with each subject:

  • I have (I've)
  • You have (You've)
  • We have (We've)
  • They have (They've)
  • He has (He's)
  • She has (She's)
  • It has (It's)
