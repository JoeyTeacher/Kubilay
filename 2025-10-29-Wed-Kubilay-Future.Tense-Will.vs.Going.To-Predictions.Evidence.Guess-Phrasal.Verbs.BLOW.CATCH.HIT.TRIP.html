Future Tenses: Will vs. Going To

Mastering the choice between will (for spontaneous offers/weak guesses) and going to (for pre-planned actions/evidence-based predictions) is key for the B1 level.

  • Spontaneous Plan/Offer (Will): "Oh, you forgot your wallet? I'll pay." (Decision made instantly).
  • Weak Guess/Feeling (Will): "I think robots will do most physical jobs by 2099." (No hard evidence, just a feeling).
  • Strong/Evidence Prediction (Going To): "She's going to fall down." (Evidence: She is standing on a toy car).
  • Pre-planned Action (Going To): "I'm going to go to a basketball game tonight with Semin." (Tickets are booked, it's a plan).

Mistake Analysis: Learning & Growing üå±

Your core focus should be on recognizing strong evidence (use going to) versus weak evidence (will).

The Functions of WILL (Spontaneous & Opinion)

Use WILL for decisions made at the moment of speaking (spontaneous) or predictions based on personal opinion/feeling (weak evidence).

Function Example from Lesson
Spontaneous Offer "Oh, you forgot your wallet? I'll pay."
Opinion / Weak Guess "I think people will be unhappy in 2099."

The Functions of GOING TO (Plan & Evidence)

Use GOING TO for actions decided before speaking (plans) or predictions based on present, physical evidence (strong evidence).

Function Example from Lesson
Pre-Planned Action "I'm going to go to the basketball game tonight."
Evidence-Based Prediction "She's going to fall down." (She is stepping on a toy.)

Phrasal Verb & Article Errors

What was said: "She's going to blow up the candles."

Correction: "She's going to blow out the candles."

What was said: "I'm going to go to a basketball game... so I'm going to go to the basketball game."

Correction: Always use a first (introducing the topic), then the (referring back). You can combine them: "I'm going to a game, so do you want to join the game?"

Teacher's Note: Practice a/the rules. If you both know the object, use the. If you are introducing it, use a.

Definitions from the Reading
