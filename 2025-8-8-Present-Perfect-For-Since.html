The 5-Minute Rule for Tackling Tasks
Have you ever felt overwhelmed by a big task? Many people have. One popular "life hack" is the "5-minute rule." This means if a task takes less than 5 minutes to do, you should do it immediately. I've personally found this incredibly helpful for small chores like washing a few dishes or sending a quick email. It's amazing how much you've accomplished by the end of the day just by applying this rule. I've recently tried this for my daily exercise routine, and it has really improved my consistency. I've been using this rule for almost three months now, and I've already seen a big difference. I haven't missed a workout yet!

The Power of a "Done List"
Most people have a "to-do list," but have you ever made a "done list"? This is where you write down everything you've already completed in a day. It's a great way to see your progress and feel motivated. I've used this method for my work projects for over a year since I started my new role, and it's always given me a boost of confidence. I've found it particularly useful when I've felt like I haven't achieved much. It helps me avoid the "same old, same old" feeling of just having a long list of things to do.
